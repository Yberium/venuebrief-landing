# VenueBrief Commercial Validation Plan

## Objective

Validate whether hospitality operators understand, want and would pay for VenueBrief before making further product or design changes.

## Rule

Do not add new features or redesign the homepage until feedback from 3-5 external contacts has been collected.

## Target contacts

Prioritise people who understand hospitality operations:

1. Restaurant managers
2. Wine bar managers
3. Small venue owners
4. Event operators
5. Hospitality consultants
6. Former colleagues who can give honest operational feedback

Avoid pitching large enterprise groups for now.

## Link to send

https://yberium.github.io/venuebrief-landing/?v=1.3

## Primary test questions

Ask these after they have opened the page:

1. What do you think this tool does?
2. Would this help a manager before service?
3. What part is unclear?
4. Would you try it for one shift?
5. Would you pay £19/month or £99 setup if it saved time?
6. What would make you say no?

## Success signals

Strong signal:
- They understand it quickly.
- They ask if it can be customised for their venue.
- They want to test it with their own staff/stations.
- They accept £99 setup or £19/month as reasonable.

Medium signal:
- They like the idea but need explanation.
- They would test it but not pay yet.
- They want a PDF/example first.

Weak signal:
- They think it is just a rota system.
- They do not understand the use case.
- They only say it looks nice but give no operational interest.

## Outreach message

Hi [Name], quick one. I’ve been building a lightweight tool for hospitality managers called VenueBrief. It turns a rota into a clear shift brief: staff, stations, handovers, warnings and a printable brief.

It’s not a rota system. It sits after the rota and makes the shift easier to run.

I’ve tested it under real service pressure and it held up well. Would you be open to having a quick look and telling me honestly if it would be useful in your venue?

https://yberium.github.io/venuebrief-landing/?v=1.3

## Follow-up message

Thanks for looking. Quick honest questions:

1. Did you understand what it does within 10 seconds?
2. Would this help before service?
3. Would you test it for one real shift?
4. Would £19/month or £99 setup feel reasonable?
5. What would stop you using it?

## Do not say yet

Avoid saying:
- It is a SaaS.
- It has AI.
- It integrates with rota systems.
- It handles HR, payroll or compliance.
- It is finished.

Say instead:
- It is an early validation tool.
- It is lightweight.
- It is designed to sit after the rota.
- It turns rota information into an operational shift brief.

## Decision after feedback

After 3-5 responses, decide:

A. If people understand it and want to test: prepare paid pilot offer.
B. If people like it but do not understand it: improve copy, not product.
C. If people understand it but would not pay: rethink positioning/pricing.
D. If people do not understand it at all: simplify landing and sample brief.
